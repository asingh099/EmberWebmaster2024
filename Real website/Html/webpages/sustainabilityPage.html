Our Environmental Mission

Also known as the Philippine lime, our calamansi is sourced from our own hydroponic farms, based in New York City. Following the guidance of farmers from the Zamboanga Peninsula in the Philippines, we perform sustainable practices like proper waste management of peels and pulp. This way of proper waste management effectively prevents greenhouse gases which they may produce.

Our milk is sourced from local cattle that are treated with the utmost care and respect, as they are considered sacred in Indian traditions. The cows live in the local grazelands of upstate New York, minimizing travel costs and our carbon footprint. Our ethical approach to dairy farming ensures healthy and stress-free cows that produce milk of the highest quality.

Sustainable Sources

We have a garden in our restaurant and use hydroponic growing technology.
Statistics:

-98% less water usage compared to traditional farms
-Maximizes space usage with vertical growing
-Independent of weather conditions (year round)
-Lower risk of contamination

Local Partnerships

We collaborate with local farmers to bring the highest quality ingredients.

Ember also buys produce including eggs, milk, and flour from local farms. The close distance requires less transportation, thus reducing our carbon footprint. It also allows us to serve the ripest food to our customers since we don't need long distance shipping and freezing. We offer a seasonal menu to give eaters an authentic local flavor. Our kitchen composts our food scraps with worms and sells it to local farmers, completing the cycle.
